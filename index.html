Menu Highlights

Each dish prepared daily from ingredients we trust, recipes we cherish.

Antipasti

  • Burrata e Prosciutto $16

    Local burrata at its creamiest, draped with aged prosciutto and served alongside grilled sourdough rubbed with good olive oil.

Primi

  • Cacio e Pepe $19

    Fresh tonnarelli pulled to order, tossed in a silky sauce of aged Pecorino Romano and coarsely cracked Tellicherry pepper. Deceptively simple, unmistakably Roman.

Secondi

  • Osso Buco $34

    Slow-braised veal shank with white wine, tomato, and gremolata. Finished table-side and served atop a mound of golden saffron risotto.

Dolci

  • Tiramisù della Nonna $11

    Grandmother Luciana's original recipe — espresso-soaked savoiardi, whipped mascarpone, and a generous dusting of dark cocoa. Made every morning. Never enough.

Our Story

Cooking the Way Nonna Did

Luciana's Table began in 2009 when Maria and Enzo Ferretti turned a lifelong dream — and a battered recipe notebook from Enzo's mother — into a trattoria the neighbourhood didn't know it needed. Fifteen years on, their daughter Sofia manages the floor while the kitchen remains fiercely, uncompromisingly a family affair.

We use Oregon's remarkable produce, proteins sourced from farms we've visited, and pasta rolled fresh every afternoon. The menu is short on purpose: everything on it is something we would serve at our own table.

No reservations needed for parties of two. For larger groups, we encourage you to call ahead — we'd love to set the table just right.
